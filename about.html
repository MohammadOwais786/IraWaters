Who We Are?

Ira Water is Pune’s leading Rainwater Harvesting provider. Since 2012 Ira is on a mission to make water available by replenishing groundwater through rainwater harvesting. Today with over 130 projects completed Ira has been a catalyst in increasing groundwater table in Western Maharashtra cities Pune, Mumbai, Nasik and Goa.

Ira has transformed << sq ft>> of landscape by recharging the acquirers and conserving 7000 million litres of water.

This year we’ve launched new product, Ira’s Water Saving Aerators that fit into existing taps. The aerators reduce the flow, regulate the pressure without affecting the water usage experience and save up to 80% of water. It is a German product, it's quality and precision make it a class-apart product.

Aims & Objectives

We started Ira with a single mission, to make water available. And our priority is to address it locally first in Pune.

We have four objectives for the period 2017-2020 that guide our work

To improve groundwater table by recharging 50,000 borewells in India

To save 3 TMC water which is equivalent to size of a dam.

To develop a sustainable model for rural water management.

To raise awareness by educating children and working with policy makers, city planners and developers.

What we seek to change?

Water availability is an ongoing issue, which is becoming more complex due to rising demand and depleting supply. In order to focus on making water available Ira has identified three key areas that must be addressed for sustenance

Attitude

Awareness, education. Basic necessity is often overlooked and taken for granted. Attitude towards water should be changed, it is a community resource, which is meant to be judiciously used, shared and conserved.

Compliance

Builders, developers, industries, new construction need to comply with the regulation so groundwater is saved from pollution and depletion.

Participation

Understand water sources and question the sustainability. We work towardsinfluencing policy makers, city planners and developers to bring about avenues for more community involvement .
